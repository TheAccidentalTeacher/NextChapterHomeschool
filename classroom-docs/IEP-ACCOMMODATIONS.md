# IEP / 504 Accommodations — ClassCiv Realms

**For:** students who need additional support. Panel finding D4.

---

## Reading level

The game UI uses middle-school vocabulary, but some prompts (treaty text, casus belli, ultimatum demand) are high-register. Accommodations:

- **Text-to-speech:** enable the browser's built-in TTS extension on student Chromebooks. Chrome's ChromeVox or the Read Aloud extension works on every prompt.
- **Peer-partner pairing:** for students who need reading support, pair them with a peer who can read prompts aloud. This is explicit in the pre-game rosters.
- **Simplified prompts:** the DM dashboard has a "Simplify Prompt" button that rephrases the current round prompt at a ~4th-grade reading level. Use it when you see a student stuck.

## Writing

Treaty text and justifications require 2+ sentences. Accommodations:

- **Speech-to-text:** enable the Chromebook's voice-typing feature (click the microphone icon in any text field). Works in every game textarea.
- **Scaffolded prompts:** the founding text field has a template ("Our civilization is called ___. It is known for ___.") that helps students who freeze at a blank screen.
- **Lowered minimum:** for students with an IEP specifying reduced written output, the DM can manually accept shorter submissions via the Override button (DMControlBar → "Accept As-Is").

## Processing time

The 60-second claim timer during Founding can feel rushed. Accommodations:

- **Extended time flag:** on the roster panel, toggle "Extended Time" for any student with a 504 or IEP covering this. Their claim window extends to 120 seconds. Other students do not see the timer extension.
- **Cohort assignment:** for Period 7's parallel-cohort draft, place students with processing-time accommodations in Cohort 2 (the longer writing window) rather than Cohort 1 (the timed draft).

## Executive function

Multiple rounds per epoch can overwhelm. Accommodations:

- **Action-queue view:** the student dashboard has a "My Turns" sidebar showing exactly one action at a time rather than the full round grid. Toggle via Settings icon (top-right).
- **Round-end reminders:** the projector fires a countdown banner 90 seconds before any round closes. This is for everyone but especially helps EF accommodations.

## Attention / regulation

Loud betrayal events on the projector are part of the game's design but can startle. Accommodations:

- **Headphones-off cue:** before a high-drama event (betrayal, war declaration), the DM can fire a short pre-event "Council Herald" animation on the projector (no audio) so students with auditory sensitivity can prepare or remove headphones if they wear them.
- **Quiet-space permission:** if a student needs to step out briefly, their civilization does not "skip a turn" — the DM marks them absent for that round and the AUTO-COVERS system assigns their action to a teammate. The student returns and picks up next round.
