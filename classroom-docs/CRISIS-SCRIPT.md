# Crisis Response Script — ClassCiv Realms

**For:** moments when a child is upset, angry, or disengaged due to an in-game event.

**Principle:** the game is supposed to have real consequences. DM Undo is a **technical repair tool**, not emotional comfort. Do not reverse a legitimate outcome just because a child is upset. The child's civilization is not erased (Guardrail 1 enforces this) — she still plays, still owns her story, still gets her portfolio.

---

## Response ladder

### Level 1 — acknowledge and reframe (most situations)

> "Your civilization just [lost a battle / was betrayed / became a vassal]. That is rough. And — your civilization is still yours. Look at your dashboard. You still pick what happens next. What do you want to do this round?"

This works 90% of the time. Naming the feeling + pointing at agency resets most students.

### Level 2 — broaden the frame

If the child is fixated on "winning":

> "Three civilizations can win — cultural, economic, and domination. You still have all three paths open. Look at your progress strips. What are you closest to?"

Point at the RulerDashboard victory strips. Every child has all three available from Epoch 1.

### Level 3 — narrative cover (only if needed)

If the child is frozen or classroom dynamic is tipping, give the event a narrative frame that preserves dignity without reversing the mechanics:

> "The council of elders met and agreed to honor your civilization's name even in defeat. The world remembers you."

You can also fire a DM global event on the projector announcing something positive about the affected civilization — a cultural achievement, a wonder in progress, a peace overture. This does not undo the game state but adds narrative weight to the losing civ.

### Level 4 — DM Undo (last resort, ONLY for actual bugs)

Use DM Undo if and only if:

- A battle resolved against the wrong target (UI misfire)
- A declaration was submitted by the wrong student (device left logged in)
- An auto-mutation violated an adjacency rule the engine should have blocked
- A DM-entered score was wrong (keyed 5, meant 3)

Do NOT use DM Undo for:

- A child who is upset about a legitimate outcome
- A child who wants a do-over on a strategic choice
- "Undoing" a public betrayal event because it was emotionally loud — that is the game working as designed

When you do use DM Undo, write a justification in the prompt (min 10 chars). The undo appears in the post-game replay.

---

## If a child leaves the room in tears

Send a second adult if possible. Follow up one-on-one after class:

- The child's portfolio is being generated regardless. Their story is not at risk.
- Ask them what they would have done differently. That conversation IS the learning.
- Do not promise to undo the game state. Do not undo.

## Parent follow-up

If a parent contacts you about their child being upset:

- Confirm the child's civilization is still in the game, still playing, still writing.
- Share the `PARENT-ONE-PAGER.md` if they did not receive it Wednesday.
- Offer to share the child's portfolio early if the arc has progressed far enough.

---

## What this document is not

This is not a script for avoiding difficult feelings in the classroom. Difficult feelings are where the learning happens. This is a script for keeping the game's integrity intact while showing the child that her civilization — and her work on it — matters.
